OS-006 Delegatic kernel

⊘ — No Cycles in Authority

Proved

Where the knowledge graph welcomes cycles — that is what κ is for — the authority graph forbids them. Delegation is acyclic by construction: no chain of "A may delegate to B" can ever loop back on itself. Privilege loops and infinite delegation chains are impossible, not merely detected.

01The claim

Let the authority graph A have an edge u → v whenever principal u may delegate to principal v. The invariant is:

∀ paths in A: no path revisits a node (A is a DAG) Enforced at write time: a delegation edge that would close a cycle is rejected before it is ever committed — not flagged on read.

This is the structural opposite of κ. The same Tarjan machinery that detects cycles in knowledge (to route to deliberation) is used here to refuse cycles in authority (to preserve the chain of responsibility).

02The evidence

delegatic/ v0.1.0 · authorization kernel

The Delegatic kernel (Policy + PolicyStore + HMAC-signed AuthorizationBlock + Authorizer) admits an authority edge only when it preserves acyclicity. Covered by the kernel's 42-test suite (25 unit + 3 integration + 14 MCP).

workbench/app/src/lib/ia/ · L14 (consume soundness)

The Workbench gate.authority proof gate projects each authorization decision into an Invariant Arithmetic value whose authority_path must be a simple path, then calls consume(value, { sigma_empty }). Law L14 (fast-check, 1000 trials) guarantees the gate passes only when the value genuinely satisfies the requirement — soundness, not optimism.

04What authority ⊘ grants

⊘ grants delegation safety: because authority cannot loop, the chain of responsibility always terminates at a root grantor. Auditing the root tells you the whole story. This is the first of four Delegatic kernel invariants — ⊘, ⊆, ⊥, ⊕ — that together make agent governance structurally honest.
