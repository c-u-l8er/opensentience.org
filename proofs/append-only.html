⊕ — Append-only Audit

Proved

Every authority decision is logged to an append-only audit stream. Past entries are immutable; the log can grow but never be rewritten. The audit trail is the proof that the other three governance invariants — ⊘, ⊆, ⊥ — were never violated along the way.

01The claim

audit log L is append-only: write(L, e) ⟹ L' = L ++ [e] (extension only) ∀ i < |L|: L'[i] = L[i] (no past entry ever changes) Sealing: each decision carries an authorization block; the chain is verifiable after the fact without trusting the writer.

02The evidence

delegatic/ v0.1.0 · authorization kernel

Authority decisions append HMAC-signed AuthorizationBlocks to the audit stream; no API mutates or deletes a prior entry. Covered by the 42-test kernel suite.

workbench/app/src/lib/ · gate.authority + redaction

In an Evidence Bundle, the authority trail is re-projected for the PRISM-facing audience and re-sealed under a content hash; redaction is idempotent and the replay harness re-verifies the chain. Tampering with a recorded decision changes the hash and fails gate.content_hash. Law L14 backs the pass/fail verdict.

03Verify it yourself

cd delegatic && mix test # append-only audit stream cd workbench/app npm test -- ia/laws.test.ts # L14 — consume soundness backs gate.authority npm test -- hash.test.ts # content-hash seal (NIST vectors)

04What authority ⊕ grants

⊕ grants non-repudiation. Because history is immutable and signed, a post-incident reviewer — human or PRISM — can reconstruct exactly which grants were in force at any moment. It is the invariant that turns the other three from promises into evidence.
