§0 The job to be done

The Workbench teaches one skill at a time with a human in the loop. The Supervisor is the next surface of the same dark-factory loop: it takes an invariant as its commission and autonomously generates, teaches, verifies, and stores a whole curriculum of skills that demonstrate that invariant across the space where it can manifest.

"I have a property my agents must satisfy. Produce a library of skills that prove the property holds, under conditions I name, with a budget I cap. Hand me back one diffable artifact that a third party can verify without running my stack."

Concretely: a teacher told "produce skills that demonstrate gate.replay_fidelity under every body × turn-count × redaction-profile combination, within a $2 / 30-minute budget" returns a CurriculumBundle whose every skill independently passes the named gate.

§1 The central invariant

This is the one line the Supervisor exists to make true — and every architectural decision serves it. A curriculum is not valid because the teacher LLM believes it covered the domain. It is valid because:

  1. every skill in the bundle independently satisfies the invariant's proof gate — the same gate processor PRISM runs on third-party submissions, so the teacher never grades its own homework;
  2. a devil's-advocate agent that did not produce the skill failed to find a cheap-pass exploit; and
  3. coverage of the declared manifestation space is reported with a Wilson lower bound the user can read — what was covered, and what is still frontier.

§2 The teacher loop

The Supervisor declares itself as a five-phase PULSE loop, identical in shape to the Graphonomous and PRISM canonical loops — so it nests cleanly inside the dual-loop architecture.

retrieve
Pull any prior CurriculumBundles for the same invariant and any prior skills tagged with it (Graphonomous retrieve).
route
Frontier check: compute manifestation_space \ visited and pick the next unvisited cell that maximizes expected information gain.
act
factory.run_one(goal, body, model) — the existing per-skill teach loop — produces one SkillBundle for the routed cell.
learn
verifyBundle(...): run the invariant's proof gate. If it passes, mark the cell covered and update confidence (Graphonomous learn).
consolidate
When budget is exhausted, the coverage target is hit, or the frontier is empty: emit the CurriculumBundle, seal it with a content hash, persist.

§3 The faculty panel

Every routed cell runs through a panel of three distinct roles. The panel is the antibody against gate weakness — but the proof gate stays authoritative: when the advocate finds a passing-but-trivial skill, that is a signal to strengthen the gate, not to veto the skill.

Lead teacher

faculty.lead

Drives the teach loop and produces the SkillBundle. It only produces — it never decides whether it passed.

Devil's advocate

faculty.advocate · advisory

Runs in a separate context and tries to expose cheap-pass, spec-gaming, or trivial passes. Sees the recorded edges and the invariant's prose — but not the gate's executable predicate.

Examiner

proof gate · authoritative

The PRISM gate processor computes the binary verdict. This is what makes a curriculum verifiable by a third party without running the teacher's stack.

§4 What it composes

The Supervisor introduces no new protocols. Its claim is that it is the only invariant-indexed teacher formulation consistent with what the [&] Stack has already shipped:

  • Periodic Table enumerates the invariants a charter can name.
  • PRISM implements gates that are invariants (gate.replay_fidelity, gate.authority, …).
  • Graphonomous implements the epistemic frontier (Wilson intervals) and coverage scoring.
  • Workbench already produces verified skill bundles.
  • PULSE declares the cross-loop tokens the teacher loop circulates.

§5 What a v0.1 Supervisor is not

  • Not a marketplace of charters — it accepts pasted JSON, full stop.
  • Not a recommender for which invariant to teach next.
  • Not multi-invariant — one invariant per charter (compositions are later).
  • Not a guarantee that every manifestation can be covered within a given budget — it reports what was covered and what is frontier-remaining.
  • Not a substitute for human curation before publication.
